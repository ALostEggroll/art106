Microbox



My rudimentary microbox made of an old Vans shoe box does two things: it plays music and can read your stress/excitement levels.


For the music portion, the box must be turned on via the big button for it to play music. The music I choose was nintendo's wii theme but while I was coding I wasn't able to get the full song. Instead I got something better in this 4 note tune that would play. Personally it was a happy little incident that I gladly kept because this tune sounded much better. The speed of the tune can be adjusted via the pot and of course the volume can be changed via amp. The 2 LED's (one top, one side) both turn on to the tune. Lastly if you press the small button, Toby Fox's Megalovania would play it's iconic intro.


For the GSR taped to the box, it works by placing your two fingers on it with one hand and it's able to read your stress/excitement levels


Overall this project was enjoyable as the struggle to put my breadbox and wires into a box felt like I was creating a ship in a bottle. The coding was a fun challenge and assembling many different components together into the breadbox was an entertaining challenge as well.
